Brick Necklaces: Icons of Resistance

June 28, 2024

Made especially for Pride 2024, these iconic brick necklaces pay homage to the radical foundations of the queer liberation movement in the historic Stonewall Riots. They promote pride in identity, inclusion, respect, and a broader capacity amongst our community and allies to recognize various sub-identities and historic subcultures. In developing this visual literacy, these necklaces open the door for us all to deepen our understanding of one another and therefore build more-resilient communities.

The brick is an icon of resistance that has taken on a nearly mythological role in the origin story of queer liberation. To me, the brick symbolizes a liberatory politics of refusal. The act of resistance encapsulated by the throwing of the brick represents a fundamental rejection of any deterministic coercion against our freedom–whether external in the form of police oppression, or internalized through oppressive self-disciplining. In this light, the brick invites us to think about not only what we refuse on the level of our being but also, importantly, what we build through that refusal.

Here, the miniaturization of the brick (made by an artisan with real concrete at a 1:6 scale) lends a kitschy, camp aesthetic and transforms this symbol into an intimate piece of jewelry that is precious like a locket. It becomes an intimate reminder, worn closely on the body, of the stakes of insisting on personal and collective freedom.

Icons throughout history often come to hold many contested meanings. For example, the crucifix has been weaponized historically to justify various atrocities, such as the medieval Crusades; and yet, that particular use does not empty out its personal significance for millions of Christians who wear it as a devotional icon of their own faith. The Pride flag as an icon is not exempt from that complexity, as a field of contested meanings.

At a time when queer identities have been co-opted by corporations for the profit of the few and weaponized through pinkwashing to justify the U.S. and Israel’s imperialist genocide in Palestine, it is crucial to recuperate the radical politics and meanings at the heart of Pride. We must wrest control of the meanings of our icons away from predatory corporate interests and warmongers. We need to move beyond merely identifying as queer–or beyond a superficial, performative allyship–and to engage with others shoulder to shoulder in building the liberated futures we all deserve.

To this end, I submit each necklace as a secular devotional icon. May these necklaces serve as a reminder of the rich legacy we have inherited as queer people, and inspire us as individuals to devote ourselves to carrying on that legacy through a personal commitment to collective struggle.
